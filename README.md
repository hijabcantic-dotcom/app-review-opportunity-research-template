# App Review Opportunity Research Template

A practical, evidence-led template for founders who want to turn public App Store and Google Play reviews into customer-interview prompts and product hypotheses.

This is not a feature-request list. The goal is to find repeated workflow failures, understand who experiences them, and decide what deserves an interview before building.

## Use it in 45 minutes

1. Pick one competitor around a single job your prospective customer already does.
2. Collect 20 to 50 recent low-rating reviews from the App Store or Google Play.
3. Paste the useful review excerpts into [`research-template.md`](research-template.md).
4. Group reviews only when they describe the same job, failure, and consequence.
5. Write one interview question for each repeated cluster. Do not treat a review as proof of demand.

## What makes a signal worth testing

Prioritize a cluster when it has all three:

- **Repetition:** multiple people independently describe the same failure.
- **Specificity:** the review names a task, context, or workaround, not just a star rating.
- **Cost:** the failure wastes time, loses work, creates risk, or blocks revenue.

Discard vague complaints, one-off bugs, and requests with no job or consequence. A repeated complaint is a lead for customer conversations, not a market-size claim.

## Suggested workflow

Start with one product. After you find a credible cluster, repeat the same exercise on two to four close alternatives. A pattern that appears across competitors is more useful than a long list of issues from one app.

For larger samples, [Review2Idea](https://review2idea.com/en/free-app-review-analysis-tool?utm_source=github&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=app_review_research_template) groups public reviews into evidence-backed pain-point clusters and produces a research starting point. Keep the source reviews and validate the hypothesis with real users.
